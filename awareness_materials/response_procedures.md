# Phishing Response Procedures

## What To Do When You Encounter Phishing

---

## Scenario 1: You Suspect a Phishing Email

### Immediate Actions

#### Step 1: STOP
**What to Do:**
- Do not click any links
- Do not download attachments
- Do not reply to the email
- Do not forward the email to others (except security team)

**Why:**
Clicking links or downloading attachments can compromise your device and the company network.

---

#### Step 2: VERIFY
**What to Do:**
- Examine the email for phishing indicators
- Check the sender's email address carefully
- Hover over links to see actual URLs
- Look for urgency tactics, generic greetings, and spelling errors

**Questions to Ask:**
- Do I recognize the sender?
- Is the domain spelled correctly?
- Was I expecting this email?
- Does the request seem unusual?

---

#### Step 3: REPORT
**What to Do:**
- Forward the email to your IT security team
- Use your organization's phishing reporting process
- Include full email headers if possible

**Where to Report:**

| Organization | Reporting Address |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Internal IT | security@company.com or IT help desk |
| Anti-Phishing WG | reportphishing@apwg.org |
| FTC (US) | reportfraud.ftc.gov |
| FBI IC3 | www.ic3.gov |
| Email Provider | Use "Report phishing" button |

---

#### Step 4: DELETE
**What to Do:**
- Delete the email from your inbox
- Empty your trash folder
- Block the sender if appropriate

**Why:**
Removing the email prevents accidental clicks in the future.

---

#### Step 5: MONITOR
**What to Do:**
- Watch your accounts for suspicious activity
- Check for unauthorized logins
- Review recent account changes
- Enable account alerts if available

---

## Scenario 2: You Clicked a Suspicious Link

### Immediate Actions

#### Step 1: DISCONNECT
**What to Do:**
- Disconnect from the internet immediately
- Unplug Ethernet cable or turn off Wi-Fi
- Do not shut down the computer yet (may lose forensic evidence)

**Why:**
Disconnecting prevents malware from communicating with command servers or spreading.

---

#### Step 2: SCAN
**What to Do:**
- Run a full antivirus/antimalware scan
- Use multiple scanning tools if available
- Check for browser extensions or plugins that were installed

**Tools:**
- Company-approved antivirus software
- Malwarebytes (for additional scanning)
- Browser security scans

---

#### Step 3: CHANGE PASSWORDS
**What to Do:**
- Change passwords for any accounts that may be compromised
- Start with email and banking accounts
- Use strong, unique passwords
- Enable MFA if not already enabled

**Priority Order:**
1. Email accounts
2. Banking and financial accounts
3. Work accounts
4. Social media accounts
5. Any accounts using the same password

---

#### Step 4: REPORT
**What to Do:**
- Notify your IT security team IMMEDIATELY
- Provide details about what was clicked
- Include the time and date of the incident
- Do not try to fix it yourself

**Information to Provide:**
- Time you clicked the link
- What the email claimed to be
- What you did after clicking
- Any pop-ups or downloads that occurred

---

#### Step 5: MONITOR
**What to Do:**
- Watch for unauthorized account access
- Review bank and credit card statements
- Check for new accounts opened in your name
- Enable credit monitoring if recommended

**For 30-90 Days:**
- Monitor accounts daily
- Watch for unusual emails or calls
- Be alert for follow-up phishing attempts

---

## Scenario 3: You Entered Credentials on a Phishing Site

### Immediate Actions

#### Step 1: CHANGE PASSWORDS IMMEDIATELY
**What to Do:**
- Change the password for the compromised account
- Change passwords for any accounts using the same password
- Enable MFA immediately

**Priority:**
1. The account whose credentials were entered
2. Email accounts (password reset capability)
3. Banking and financial accounts
4. Work accounts
5. Other accounts with same/similar passwords

---

#### Step 2: CONTACT THE SERVICE PROVIDER
**What to Do:**
- Contact the real company (not through the phishing site)
- Report that your credentials may be compromised
- Ask them to monitor your account for suspicious activity
- Consider requesting a temporary account lock

---

#### Step 3: REPORT TO SECURITY TEAM
**What to Do:**
- Notify IT security immediately
- Provide the phishing site URL if known
- Report what credentials were entered
- Follow your organization's incident response procedures

---

#### Step 4: MONITOR FOR IDENTITY THEFT
**What to Do:**
- Check credit reports
- Enable fraud alerts
- Consider credit freeze if recommended
- Watch for unauthorized account openings

---

## Scenario 4: You Received a BEC (Business Email Compromise) Request

### Immediate Actions

#### Step 1: DO NOT PROCESS THE REQUEST
**What to Do:**
- Stop immediately if you haven't processed it yet
- Do not transfer any funds
- Do not provide sensitive information

---

#### Step 2: VERIFY THROUGH SECOND CHANNEL
**What to Do:**
- Call the executive using a known phone number
- Do not use contact information from the email
- Verify the request in person if possible

**Important:**
Never use phone numbers or contact information from suspicious emails.

---

#### Step 3: REPORT TO MANAGEMENT
**What to Do:**
- Notify your manager immediately
- Contact the CFO or finance director
- Report to the security team
- Document the incident

---

#### Step 4: PRESERVE EVIDENCE
**What to Do:**
- Do not delete the email
- Save email headers
- Take screenshots
- Document the timeline

## Prevention Reminders

### Daily Security Habits

1. **Think Before You Click**
   - Take 5 seconds to examine emails
   - Hover over links before clicking
   - Verify sender addresses

2. **When in Doubt, Verify**
   - Contact sender through trusted channels
   - Follow up on unusual requests
   - Escalate to security team

3. **Keep Security Updated**
   - Enable automatic updates
   - Use MFA on all accounts
   - Keep antivirus active

4. **Stay Informed**
   - Attend security awareness training
   - Read security bulletins
   - Learn about new phishing tactics
