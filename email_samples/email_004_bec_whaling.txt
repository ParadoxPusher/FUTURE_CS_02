[CRITICAL] Unusual Request: Large wire transfer ($47,500) without proper 
           documentation or approval chain

[HIGH] Extreme Urgency: Multiple urgency indicators - "URGENT", 
       "time-sensitive", "must be completed today"

[HIGH] Email Authentication: No DKIM, SPF FAILED

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ATTACK EXPLANATION:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is a Business Email Compromise (BEC) attack - the most financially 
damaging type of phishing. The attacker impersonates the CEO to request an 
urgent wire transfer, bypassing normal approval processes. 

BEC attacks have caused BILLIONS in losses globally. The "Sent from my iPhone" 
signature adds plausibility for the brief message and urgency.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
REMEDIATION:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. NEVER process wire transfers based solely on email requests
2. ALWAYS verify through a second channel (phone call to known number)
3. Follow established approval procedures - NO EXCEPTIONS
4. Be suspicious of "urgent" requests to bypass protocols
5. Report immediately to security team and CFO
