PHISHING INDICATORS IDENTIFIED:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[CRITICAL] Typosquatting Domain: 'paypa1-secure.com' uses number '1' instead 
           of letter 'l'

[HIGH] Suspicious Link: URL 'paypal-secure-verification.com' is not the 
       official paypal.com domain

[MEDIUM] Urgency Tactics: Uses fear-based language - "permanently suspended", 
         "24 hours"

[LOW] Generic Greeting: Uses "Dear Customer" instead of recipient's name

[HIGH] Email Authentication: DKIM and SPF checks both FAILED

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ATTACK EXPLANATION:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is a typosquatting attack where the attacker registers a domain that 
looks similar to PayPal (paypa1 vs paypal). The email creates urgency by 
threatening account suspension, hoping victims will click the link and enter 
their credentials on a fake login page controlled by attackers.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
REMEDIATION:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. NEVER click links in emails claiming account problems
2. Always navigate directly to paypal.com by typing in your browser
3. Check your account status by logging in directly on the official website
4. Report phishing emails to reportphishing@apwg.org
